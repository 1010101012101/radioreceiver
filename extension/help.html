Using Radio Receiver

Start and stop

Press the "Power On" button (1) to start playing the radio. You'll see that the label changes to "Power Off"; if you press it again, the radio will stop.

Changing frequency

You can change frequency in several ways:

  • Click on the frequency display (2), type your new frequency directly, and press Enter.
  • Use the "<" and ">" buttons (3) to change the frequency 1 step up or down.
  • Use the "Scan <<" and "Scan >>" buttons (4) to search for a station automatically.
  • Use the mouse wheel on the frequency display.

Using presets

Click the "Save" button (5) to save the station you are listening to as a preset. Enter a name for the preset and press "Save".

To switch to a preset, click the preset selection box (6) and choose your new preset. The radio will switch to it immediately.

To delete a preset, switch to it and press the "Remove" button (7).

To change a preset's name, switch to it and click the "Save" button (5). Type the new name and press "Save".

Changing volume and stereo

To change the volume, click on the "loudspeaker" icon (8) and move the slider left and right to decrease or increase the volume. You can also use your mouse wheel on the icon to change volume directly.

To disable stereo click on the stereo indicator (9).

Settings

You can change a lot of settings by clicking on the "wrench" icon (10). There you can change several settings:

  • Region: Radios use different frequencies in different parts of the world. For best results, you should select the part of the world you live in.
  • Frequency correction: Corrects for clock imprecision in the dongle. Most of the time you can leave it at 0 unless you know you need a different value. If you already know it, enter it here. Otherwise, you can try the "suggest a value" feature.
  • Tuner gain: Allows you to use automatic gain or set a custom fixed gain. Most people can use automatic gain.
